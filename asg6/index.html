StudyScape — Six Thinking Hats Summary

Overview

Partnering with a friend outside the course, I reviewed my StudyScape project—a simple website that maps quiet or visually engaging study locations on and around campus—using the Six Thinking Hats method. Below are short summaries for each hat and how the project changed afterward.

White Hat

We listed the facts and what's missing. StudyScape shows study spots with filters such as “outdoor,” “with outlets,” and “coffee nearby,” plus ratings and photo uploads. We still need info like which areas are most popular, how often students study outside, and which filters they would actually use so the design stays useful.

Red Hat

We shared feelings. My friend felt good about the idea because it supports calm and focus. I felt excited and proud, but a little worried about long-term adoption. We agreed the aesthetic angle could encourage sharing because students enjoy showcasing favorite spots.

Black Hat

We looked for risks: incorrect or messy listings without moderation, popular spots losing their quiet vibe, and possible accessibility gaps. This means I should plan simple upload rules, basic review checks, and clear accessibility notes.

Yellow Hat

We noted benefits: saving time and stress between classes, helping new students explore campus, and easy access because it’s web-based (no downloads). The main strengths are simplicity and real usefulness.

Green Hat

We brainstormed: a “Study Mood” filter (quiet, cozy, bright), optional crowd/noise hints, small badges for verified contributions, and short reviews or playlists for vibe. These make the site more fun while staying simple.

Blue Hat

We organized next steps: short student survey, low-fi wireframes, early moderation guidelines, and a calm visual style. This turns ideas into a basic plan I can follow.

Project Evolution

After the conversation, I expanded StudyScape from a simple map into a small, student-led community with light moderation and accessibility notes. I will prototype the filters, test with a few students, and adjust based on feedback.
